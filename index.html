🌐 Foreign Lobbying Disclosure (FARA)

Official government data

What this is: Under the Foreign Agents Registration Act (1938), anyone in the U.S. representing a foreign government, political party, or foreign-controlled entity for political or public-relations purposes must register with the Department of Justice and disclose who they represent.

Why it matters: This is the primary public record of who is being paid to influence U.S. policy, media, or public opinion on behalf of a foreign interest.

How to read it: "Active" means the registration is currently in effect. "Terminated" means the agent formally ended the relationship or stopped required activity — it does not by itself imply wrongdoing.
