Foreign Agents Registration Act

Foreign lobbying (FARA)

Official record
What is this?
Anyone in the U.S. paid by a foreign government, a foreign political party, or a company controlled by one, to influence American politics, media, or public opinion has to register with the Department of Justice and say who's paying them.
Why care?
It's the only public list of who's being paid by a foreign interest to shape U.S. policy or opinion — and for whom.
How to read it
Active means the registration is currently in effect. Terminated means the agent told the government they stopped — on its own, that's routine, not a red flag.

Lobbying Disclosure Act

U.S. lobbying (LDA)

Official record
What is this?
Anyone paid to lobby Congress or a federal agency on behalf of a client has to register and file a report every few months on what they lobbied for.
Why care?
It's the public paper trail of who's trying to influence federal law, for which client, and on which issue — separate from campaign donations.
How to read it
A high filing count in one quarter is normal — every registrant reports on the same schedule, so this isn't a lobbying "spike."

Federal Election Commission

Campaign finance (FEC)

Official record
What is this?
Groups that raise and spend money in federal elections — parties, PACs, and "Super PACs" — have to register with the FEC and file financial reports.
Why care?
It's the master list of organized money around federal elections: how many of these operations exist and how active they are.
How to read it
A Super PAC can raise and spend unlimited money independently, but by law can't give directly to a candidate. The counts below are how many committees and reports exist — not dollar totals (see the note below).

Automatic license-plate readers

Plate cameras (ALPR)

Independent research — not official
What is this?
Cameras — often made by a company called Flock Safety — that scan and log every license plate that passes, whether or not it's linked to a crime. This tracks which police agencies have been documented using them.
Why care?
No government keeps a public list of where these cameras are. This is the most rigorously sourced public accounting that exists, built by researchers checking news reports and public records — not police self-reporting.
How to read it
Treat this as "at least this many, probably more." Coverage reflects how much researchers have found, not real-time reality — see the caveats below.
